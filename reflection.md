# Spokane Cyber Cup Hackathon Reflection

## Activity Description

The Spokane Cyber Cup Hackathon was a CTF event where people work on security challenges and problem-solving exercises. The event encouraged people to apply cybersecurity knowledge in a practical setting while working with other students and professionals.

My expectation for the event was to gain more skills in solving cyber challenges in a competitive setting. During the hackathon, I mainly only interacted with my team, explored the different challenges presented. Although the event was time-limited and did not result in a polished project deliverable, it provided exposure to cyber challenges and helped me expand my knowledge base.

## Technical Decisions

From a technical perspective, the event focused analytical thinking and problem decomposition. One important factor when approaching challenges was figuring out whether a problem should be solved through manual analysis or with self made scripts.

Another technical consideration was prioritization. This hackathon provided many different challenges at different skill levels. Focusing on smaller problems early allowed us to gain momentum and build confidence when tackling the more complex tasks.

## Contributions

I participated in a team for the Cyber Club here at. My contributions mainly were OSINT challenges and data analysis with some pattern recognition challenges too. These scaling challenges helped refine my problem-solving strategies and exposed me to different perspectives on cyber challenges.

## Quality Assessment

Overall, I believe my participation in the Spokane Cyber Cup Hackathon was valuable for gaining exposure to cybersecurity competition environments. The event allowed me to practice analytical thinking and interact with other students interested in cybersecurity.

If I were to participate again, I would prepare more beforehand by practicing additional challenges and becoming more familiar with commonly used tools.
